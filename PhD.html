PhD

The Department of Computer Science (DCS) at the University of Ghana (UG) offers a Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) program in Computer Science. The program is designed for students who wish to pursue a research degree in computer science and make significant contributions to the field. The PhD program is a full-time program that typically takes four years to complete. Students are required to take coursework, conduct research, and write a dissertation.

Coursework

Students are required to take a core set of courses that cover a wide range of topics in computer science, including: Algorithms, Data structures, Programming languages, Operating systems, Computer architecture, Computer networks, Artificial intelligence, Computer graphics, Computer security, Database systems, Software engineering, Web development

Coursework Students are required to take a core set of courses that cover a wide range of topics in computer science, including: Algorithms Data structures Programming languages Operating systems Computer architecture Computer networks Artificial intelligence Computer graphics Computer security Database systems Software engineering Web development

In addition to the core courses, students are also required to take elective courses that are relevant to their research interests.

Research

Students are expected to conduct original research under the supervision of a faculty member. The research should be publishable in a peer-reviewed journal or conference.

Dissertation

The dissertation is the culmination of the PhD program. It is a publishable document that makes a significant contribution to the field of computer science.
